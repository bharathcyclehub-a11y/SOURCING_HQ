## SECTION 7 — STRUCTURAL FINDINGS (read this before calling anyone)

### What the data revealed about the Indian RC market:

1. **Hobby-grade retail is genuinely thin.** Across all of South + West + North India, fewer than **15 genuinely hobby-grade specialist stores** exist with verified active operations. This is not a saturated market.

2. **Mumbai owns hobby-grade national distribution.** Hemani Exim + MRC Hobbies + Hobby Central + Bharat Hobby + Daddy Drones + @rcinc.in = Mumbai is the brain of Indian hobby RC. Bangalore (Havoc Hobby + Modelart + Edall + RACEiN PARC + FuryRoad) is #2.

3. **Delhi has NO authorized Traxxas storefront.** The Karol Bagh "Traxxas India" Justdial listing is single-source and likely outdated. The real Traxxas India social handle (@traxxasindia) routes to @rcinc.in in Mumbai. **Confirmed: Konnect Enterprises Rampur is defunct in RC.**

4. **North India hobby-grade is structurally weak.** Adventure Wheels (Gurgaon, Tamiya), Fire Birds (Jalandhar), RC Racing Hobbies (Delhi/HPI), Sharma Models (Jodhpur, aero) is essentially the entire verified North hobby ecosystem. The rest are toy wholesalers misclassified as hobby on Justdial.

5. **Repair service is a documented gap nationwide.** Multiple rcindia.org threads complain about no good RC car repair in Delhi. **BCH could position Bangalore as the courier-in service hub for North India** — defensible competitive angle.

6. **North/east Bangalore = total RC retail vacuum.** Every hobby retailer in BLR is south/central (Shantinagar, Koramangala, Jayanagar, JP Nagar, Chickpet). BCH's geographic position is BCH's biggest asset.

7. **The bridge tier (₹15-25K) is unfilled.** Havoc Hobby stocks ARRMA at ₹36K entry and toy-grade ₹2-5K. MJX Hyper Go at ₹16-20K is the missing rung BCH can own.
