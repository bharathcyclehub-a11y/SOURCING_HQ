## Hard rules (do not break)

1. **Don't promise a purchase order on your first call.** You're discovering, not buying. Your line is: *"We're a Bangalore retailer evaluating the RC category for next quarter. Can you share your catalog and wholesale rate list on WhatsApp so I can take it back to my owner?"*
2. **Don't share Syed's personal phone with vendors.** Use your own number. If a vendor insists on talking to the owner, say *"He'll call you back if we shortlist you — first I need the catalog and rates."*
3. **Don't pay any factory before Syed signs off.** Sample orders ₹3K–5K total are OK on your card — keep the receipt, send Syed the photo same day. Anything above ₹5K = WhatsApp Syed first.
4. **No Justdial. No IndiaMART for verification.** (Use IndiaMART only for breadth — to discover names. Never trust their numbers as proof a vendor is real.) Verify every vendor on **Google Maps** before trusting them. Reason: Justdial/IndiaMART have stale lead-gen listings — half are dead phone numbers, half are call-center middlemen. Maps + Instagram + LinkedIn + company website are the only trustworthy verification stack.
5. **Don't waste time sourcing from Sadar Bazar Delhi.** That cluster is **toy-grade** (sub-₹500 plastic) — we verified visually. Only go to Sadar Bazar for **competitor price intelligence** (so you know what kid toys retail at, which sets the floor for our pricing). Don't put a Sadar Bazar vendor on the shortlist.
6. **Confirm everything in writing on WhatsApp.** Phone-call commitments evaporate. After every call: *"Bhai, jo rate aapne bola, WhatsApp pe catalog + price list bhej do please."* No WhatsApp confirmation = the call didn't happen.
7. **Don't book Mumbai/Delhi travel in week 1.** You need the phone call data first to know who's worth visiting. Travel decisions go through Syed.
8. **Don't visit a vendor alone if you haven't spoken to them on the phone first.** No cold door-knocks except for Taiba Technoplast and Itoyy Kiing — those two have no public phone, door-knock is the only path (and even then, Syed will tell you when).

## Quick glossary

- **Hobby-grade RC** = ₹15K+, brushless motor, 1:10 scale, hobbyist tier (Tygatec, Traxxas, MJX premium, ARRMA). NOT our SKU.
- **Toy-grade RC** = ₹99–500, plastic kid toys (Sadar Bazar Delhi cluster). NOT our SKU.
- **Grey-space drift RC** = ₹800–2,500 retail — our SKU. Looks hobby-ish (LED lights, licensed body shape, drift wheels), priced for mass-market gifting and impulse.
- **CIF Mumbai** = Cost-Insurance-Freight delivered to Mumbai port (the landed cost before Indian duties are added).
- **MOQ** = Minimum Order Quantity (how many units per SKU per order the vendor will accept).
- **BIS** = Bureau of Indian Standards — mandatory certification for imported toys. ₹50K one-time per SKU. Importer pays.
- **HS-95030030** = the Indian customs code for "remote-controlled toys including remote cars." If a vendor doesn't recognize this code, they're not a real importer.
- **JNPT** = Jawaharlal Nehru Port (Mumbai) — where 65% of India's RC containers land.
- **Bhiwandi** = the warehouse cluster (25M sq ft) outside Mumbai where most of the inventory sits.
- **1:24 scale / 1:43 scale** = the size ratio. 1:24 = palm-sized, 1:43 = pocket-sized. Both viral on Instagram.
- **Private label** = vendor manufactures it, we put our brand on it. Skips import duty if made in India.
