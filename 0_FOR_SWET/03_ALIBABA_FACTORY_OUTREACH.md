## CONTEXT YOU NEED TO HAVE IN YOUR HEAD BEFORE YOU DM ANYONE

1. **The SKU we're chasing:** 1:14 to 1:18 drift RC car, brushless or brushed, with LEDs + drift wheels + popular car-shell licences (GTR/Supra/BMW M3/Lambo). Retail band ₹800–₹2,500. This is the grey-space SKU between toy and hobby.
2. **The benchmark price they're paying:** Average **$2.00 CIF Mumbai per RC unit** under HS 95030090 (Volza/Zauba-aggregated, FY24-25). Stunt-car median is even lower ($0.90). That's our cost target.
3. **The India duty stack:** **~109% total tax on CIF** = BCD 70% + SWS (7% effective) + IGST 18% on (CIF+BCD+SWS). IGST is recoverable as Input Tax Credit since BCH is GST-registered. Real sunk cost = BCD + SWS ≈ 77% of CIF.
4. **BIS QCO certification:** **₹3–5 lakh one-time per SKU**, takes 3–4 months. Mandatory for electric toys under IS 9873 / IS 15644. This is a Syed-level decision, not a Swet-level worry — but mention it to the factory because they have to share test reports + lab samples.
5. **The Chenghai context:** Chenghai District in Shantou (Guangdong) makes ~70% of the world's plastic toys, ~4,000 toy factories. The 3 industrial parks to name-drop on chat: **Xiehe, Lianhua, Wenguan**. Sounding informed gets you better prices.

## How to vet a Chinese factory before placing a sample order (CRITICAL — do not skip)

This is a checklist. Tick every box before you ask Syed to approve a sample payment. A scammy factory will fail at least 3 of these — and the sample order itself can be the scam (charge you, ship nothing).

- [ ] **Gold Supplier badge present + 5+ years on Alibaba.** (Less than 2 years = high risk.)
- [ ] **Trade Assurance enabled.** Alibaba escrows your money — they release it to the factory only after dispatch and quality confirmation. Never pay outside Trade Assurance for the first order.
- [ ] **Verified Manufacturer (not Trading Company).** If trading company, get the factory name + location and search that factory separately.
- [ ] **Video call / video tour requested.** Ask: "Can you do a 5-minute WhatsApp video tour of your facility?" A real factory will say yes. A reseller will dodge.
- [ ] **Production-line photos with a date marker.** Ask them to hold a paper with today's date in one production-line photo. Confirms current capacity.
- [ ] **Reviews on Alibaba — read the bad ones first.** Filter to 1- and 2-star reviews and read the complaints. Late shipping = manageable; broken units / no response = walk away.
- [ ] **Cross-check on Volza.** Search the factory name on volza.com — if they show up as a supplier to other Indian / US / EU importers with multiple shipments, they're real. If zero export records, that's a red flag for a brand-new entity.
- [ ] **BIS test report willingness.** Ask: "Can you provide IS 9873 / IS 15644 test reports, or supply lab samples for Indian BIS testing?" Serious factories say yes — they've done it for other India-bound clients. A blank-stare response means they've never exported to India and the certification work will fall on us.

If a factory fails 3+ of these, drop them and move to the next on your shortlist.

## When you tell the factory we want BIS / IS 9873 compliance — what they need to do

This is the technical part Syed will care about. Have the factory confirm:

1. They can supply **physical samples to an Indian BIS-approved lab** (TUV India, Intertek India, etc.) for IS 9873 / IS 15644 testing.
2. They will not change the bill of materials between sample and production batch (BIS certifies one specific construction — any change = recertification).
3. They will provide a **material safety datasheet** (phthalate, lead, cadmium levels for the plastic + paint).
4. They will provide an **EMC / radio compliance report** for the 2.4 GHz transmitter (mandatory for BIS QCO).

If the factory cannot do any of these, the BIS path becomes our problem to manage end-to-end — flag that to Syed as it adds ~₹50K to the BIS budget per SKU.

## Common Alibaba scams to avoid (read this once, then act paranoid)

- **The "switch the bank account" scam.** Factory replies look legit for 2 weeks, then sends an email saying "our bank changed, please pay to this new account." Always pay through Trade Assurance, never to a side account.
- **The "trading company pretending to be the factory" pattern.** They quote a sharp price, then add costs after you commit. Counter: ask for the factory address, then check the Alibaba storefront's business licence (visible on the profile).
- **The "sample is perfect, production is junk" pattern.** Sample is cherry-picked. Counter: in production, **demand a pre-shipment inspection (PSI)** by a third party (QIMA, SGS — ₹15K–₹25K per inspection). Syed will sign off on this — make sure the line item is in your PO.
- **The "vanishing chat" pattern.** Great responses for 3 days, then radio silence. Counter: any factory that can't respond within 24 hours during business days is not serious. Move on.
