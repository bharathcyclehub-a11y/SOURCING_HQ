## THE CORE INSIGHT

> A ₹65 sunglass bought in Surat Katargam becomes a ₹1,000 product in a glass display case. The product didn't change. The context did.

This is not a trick. This is how Boat (valued ₹2,200 Cr) started. How Chumbak raised ₹25 Cr. How Phool sells temple flowers for 20x. How every Fabindia kurta works. The same physical object in a different context = a different product.

**The 3-Layer Arbitrage Stack:**
1. **Price arbitrage** — Buy at ₹30–100, retail at ₹300–1000 (5–10x base)
2. **Packaging arbitrage** — Add ₹40–80 in box/tissue/card, charge ₹500 more (pushes to 8–12x)
3. **Narrative arbitrage** — Add a story ("handcrafted," "GI-tagged," "artisan family"), add another 30–50%

**Channel Tags used throughout:**
- **[PHYSICAL]** — needs touch, display, impulse context (glass case, boutique shelf, hotel gift shop)
- **[D2C]** — photographs well, ships easily, Instagram/content-native
- **[BOTH]** — strong in either channel

## PART 6: PRACTICAL BUYING GUIDE

### How to Unlock True Wholesale Prices
1. **Never ask for single price** — Always ask "dozen price" (12 pieces). It signals you're a trade buyer.
2. **Carry cash** — Physical cash gets 10–25% additional discount universally.
3. **Visit Tuesday–Thursday** — Avoid Monday (restocking chaos), Friday (religious), Sunday (closed).
4. **Use Hindi, not English** — English triggers tourist pricing in most markets.
5. **Ask for the godown, not the shop** — Godown prices are 20–30% cheaper than street-facing shops.
6. **Quote your destination market** — "Main Bangalore mein bechta hoon" = more respect, sometimes better price.

### Minimum Order Quantities (Realistic)

## PART 7: THE PSYCHOLOGY OF PREMIUM (WHY CUSTOMERS PAY 10X)

Ranked by power:

1. **Social signaling (Most powerful)** — The buyer is purchasing a signal to send to their social circle, not the product. This is why packaging is not a nice-to-have — it IS the core product. "I got this from a cool brand" = real value to the buyer.

2. **Context transfer** — A product in a premium context borrows that premium. Your sunglass in a glass case = premium sunglass. Same item on the pavement = ₹65 sunglass.

3. **Narrative** — "Fired in a 150-year-old wood kiln by Ramji Kumbhar's family" adds ₹300–500 vs. "handmade pottery." Specificity = premium.

4. **Packaging as first impression** — Peak-end rule: people remember the peak experience. Premium packaging creates the peak moment. 68% of D2C repeat purchases cite unboxing experience (McKinsey India, 2022).

5. **Influencer association** — Micro-influencers (10K–100K followers) in India deliver better ROI than macro — audience trust is higher. For a product costing ₹150, 50 influencer gifting units (₹7,500 COGS) can generate ₹5L+ in demand.

6. **Scarcity signals** — "Only 3 left," "DM to order," "Limited batch" — loss aversion at work. "DM to order" removes the price from the feed entirely, creates perceived exclusivity.
