This means BCH's three real paths for the ₹10K-15K bracket are NOT "find an existing distributor" — they are:

## SECTION 1 — WHY THIS BRACKET IS EMPTY (the market explanation)

| Brand tier | India distribution model | Entry price (India) |
|------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Premium (Kyosho/ARRMA/Traxxas/Tamiya/FMS/HPI) | Single exclusive distributor (Hemani Exim, Adventure Wheels, RC Racing Hobbies) | **₹22-30K+** |
| Bridge (MJX/WL/Rlaarlo/Hobby Plus/JLB/HSP) | "Anyone can be a distributor" — Alibaba/1688 direct | ₹10-20K |
| Gray-zone (Toofun/NIYAMAT/Pepstter) | Marketplace sellers + Sadar wholesale | ₹1-3K |
| Indian D2C (LOT/Tygatec/Mirana/HGT) | Brand-owner direct, no traditional dealers | ₹1-9K (LOT) to ₹10K (Tygatec) |

**Why no single brand owns this price tier with India exclusivity:**

1. **Premium brands won't go below ₹22K** — their margin model + Hemani Exim's distributor cost structure require it
2. **Chinese bridge brands DELIBERATELY avoid exclusive distributors** — their growth strategy is volume + multi-channel, not gated access (see [MJX_INDIA_DISTRIBUTOR_HUNT.md](MJX_INDIA_DISTRIBUTOR_HUNT.md) Section 4)
3. **Indian D2C brands haven't scaled to ₹15K yet** — they're winning the ₹2-5K range first, premium tier is a roadmap item

The result: **the brand that "owns" the ₹10-15K shelf in India is whichever retailer (Daddy Drones, Hobby Central) has the inventory at any moment** — not a brand.

**This is the OPPORTUNITY.** BCH can become a brand's official Bangalore arm before the brand even formalizes Indian distribution.
