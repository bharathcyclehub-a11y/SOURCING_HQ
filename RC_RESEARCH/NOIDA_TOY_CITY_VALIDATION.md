# Noida "Toy City" — Validation & RC Manufacturer Audit

**Date:** 2026-05-24
**For:** BCH RC car sourcing
**Sources:** YEIDA, ThePrint, India TV, Toy Association of India, Outlook Business

---

## VERDICT

**Half-real, mostly hype for our use-case.** Two distinct "Toy City" entities exist near Greater Noida, but **neither has a single hobby-grade RC manufacturer**. The new YEIDA Toy Park (Sector 33) is largely a land-allotment story — 140 plots given, only 2–3 under construction five years in. The older Toy City (Udyog Kendra, Ecotech-III) is mostly plush, dolls, ride-ons, and PVC battery-op toy-grade SKUs.

---

## 1. What "Toy City" actually means

There are **two clusters**, often conflated:

| Cluster | Location | Year | Size | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Old Toy City** | Udyog Kendra, Ecotech-III, Greater Noida (pin 201306) | Late 1990s | ~100+ plots | Operational, mature, mostly soft/plastic toy-grade |
| **New YEIDA Toy Park** | Sector 33, Yamuna Expressway (near Jewar airport) | Announced 2020 | 100 acres, 140 plots, Rs 410 cr committed | **Largely stalled** — only Funzoo under construction as of 2025 ([ThePrint](https://theprint.in/india/governance/why-yeidas-toy-park-is-struggling-to-take-off-despite-up-govt-push-and-strategic-location/2735431/)) |

YEIDA Sector 33 is the one the government keeps publicising. **Reality check from ThePrint (2025):** 140 plots allotted, 84 lease deeds executed, 58 took possession, **2–3 factories under construction, zero operational**. Allottees blame missing electricity/water, US tariffs killing the export thesis, and suspected land speculation tied to Jewar airport ([source](https://theprint.in/india/governance/why-yeidas-toy-park-is-struggling-to-take-off-despite-up-govt-push-and-strategic-location/2735431/)).

The Rs 410 cr / 6,157 jobs / 134 companies number repeated in [India TV](https://www.indiatvnews.com/business/news-noida-toy-park-manufacturing-hub-134-companies-gets-land-uttar-pradesh-724866) and [TheNewsAgency](https://www.thenewsagency.in/uttar-pradesh/noida-set-to-be-toy-manufacturing-hub-134-companies-acquire-land-in-toy-park-to-invest-rs-410-crore) is a **land-allotment** figure, not a deployed-capex figure.

## 3. Hobby-grade in Noida?

**No.** Confirmed via Toy Association of India member directory ([tai-india.org](https://tai-india.org/aboutus/meetofficebearers)) — no member is listed making brushless 1:10/1:12, LiPo-powered, Ackerman-steering cars. President Ajay Aggarwal's industry pitch is soft toys + educational + board games for export under PLI ([MyIndMakers](https://myind.net/Home/viewArticle/indian-toy-manufacturers-in-the-midst-of-a-golden-period-toy-association-of-india-president), [Outlook Business](https://www.outlookbusiness.com/economy-and-policy/tariff-war-opens-door-for-india-to-emerge-as-global-toy-export-hub)). RC is not in the Indian toy-PLI conversation.

## 5. Anchor tenants / Chinese JVs at YEIDA Toy Park

**None announced.** The original 2020 pitch by UP CM Yogi mentioned Chinese partners, but ThePrint's 2025 investigation found **no JV announcements, no anchor brand, no Funskool/Hamleys/Hasbro tie-up** ([ThePrint](https://theprint.in/india/governance/why-yeidas-toy-park-is-struggling-to-take-off-despite-up-govt-push-and-strategic-location/2735431/)). Hamleys' India play is retail (Reliance-owned), not manufacturing. Funskool's three factories are in Goa, Ranipet, and Hyderabad — not Noida ([funskoolindia.com/our-factories](https://funskoolindia.com/our-factories/)).

## TOP 5 LEADS TO ACTUALLY CHASE (ranked by RC relevance)

1. **Emergance Exim India Pvt Ltd** (Greater Noida) — manufactures **PCBs for RC toy cars**. Highest-signal Noida contact for understanding the toy-grade RC electronics supply chain. Could be a backdoor into who they sell to.
2. **KV Toys India Ltd** — only Noida-belt name actually making RC-labeled product (toy-grade). Worth one call to map the toy-grade landscape before you compete with them upstream.
3. **Daddy Drones (Mumbai)** — most credible hobby-grade retailer with spares + after-sales. Best benchmark for what hobby-grade pricing/margins look like in India.
4. **Havoc Hobby (Bangalore, Shanthinagar)** — local to you, hobby-grade, services Indian + international. Easy in-person scout to see what 1:10 brushless actually retails at and who their import partners are.
5. **Toy Association of India — Ajay Aggarwal (President)** — one call answers whether *any* member is moving into hobby-grade, plus an intro to the Toy Biz International fair exhibitor list for vendor scouting.

**Strategic takeaway:** Noida Toy City is a real-estate story, not a manufacturing story — and even the manufacturing that does happen is toy-grade plush/plastic. The hobby-grade RC category in India is **100% import-dependent today**. That's the gap, and that's the moat if BCH chooses to build a domestic hobby-grade SKU.

### 6f. Hobby-grade retail in Delhi-NCR — the gap stays a gap

**No dedicated hobby-grade RC storefront exists in Delhi NCR.** RC Sky Hobby (Patparganj) is a trader. Bharat Hobby and Havoc both ship in from Mumbai/Bangalore. The [RC India forum Noida/Delhi thread](https://www.rcindia.org/rc-locations-and-clubs/rc-car-enthusiasts-in-noidadelhi/) confirms enthusiasts route purchases via Mumbai/Bangalore or import direct. **This is a demand gap, not just a supply gap** — Delhi NCR is the largest metro RC market with no flagship physical store.
