## TL;DR — the answer

**Maharashtra is the biggest state for grey-space drift RC sourcing.** Three independent data sources converge:

1. **Maharashtra owns 65% of India's RC car imports** by container count (Taiba Technoplast Mumbai 42% + Itoyy Kiing Bhiwandi 23%, both partnership firms, JNPT-port-fed). Delhi is #3 at 12% via Fantom Toy Sadar Bazar.
2. **Maharashtra is the only state with the full stack** — port (JNPT/Mundra adjacency) + warehousing (Bhiwandi 25M sq ft + 7 Amazon FCs) + hobby retail (21 RCDC, MRC Hobbies, Hobby Central) + funded RC brands (Daddy Drones, Mirana Pune, Hemani/Kyosho). It's where containers land *and* where the grey-space SKU finally walks out to consumers.
3. **The PANDAY benchmark independently validates this** — 7 of your top 11 ranked brands are MH-based (Daddy Drones, Mirana, Hobby Central, Bharat Hobby, Hemani Exim, rcbazaar, MRC Hobbies).

**But the answer depends on what part of the stack you want to anchor in:**

| Your move | Best state | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct-import from China** (cheapest landed cost) | **Maharashtra** | JNPT port, Bhiwandi warehouses, Taiba + Itoyy already prove the model. 3 Chinese factories (Shantou Maxwinning + Yiwu Ying Hong + Tianjin Textiles) feed 77% of India RC imports — go around them via Alibaba. |
| **Wholesale B2B walk-and-bargain** (small-lot price discovery) | **Delhi NCR** | Sadar Bazar Teliwara cluster — V.G. Marketing, Fantom Toy, Iskon, Kamal all within 200m. But product is **toy-grade**, not grey-space — buyer beware. |
| **Make-in-India private-label** (skip imports, dodge 70% duty) | **Gujarat** | Toyzone Ahmedabad is the only Indian RC manufacturer at scale (₹500–₹3,000 band, X Power RC at ~₹1,500, 36 lakh+ Magic Cars sold lifetime). Plus your existing Surat vendor base + Mundra port. |
| **Competitive intel on hobby ecosystem** | **Karnataka + Telangana (joint)** | These two states have the deepest verified hobby-shop ecosystem on Google Maps (Karnataka 8 vendors, Telangana 8 vendors) — outpacing Maharashtra (5) and Delhi (2) on that specific metric. RC Trendsetters Hyderabad has **3,007 Maps reviews** = the deepest social proof in India. |

**Recommended state to anchor in: Maharashtra (Mumbai–Bhiwandi corridor), Gujarat (Ahmedabad–Surat) as backup for manufacturing.**

## What the import economics tell us about the SKU you're chasing

- **HS code 95030030** ("remote-controlled toys including remote cars") = the right tax bucket. India imported ~$84.7M in FY24-25 of all toys, ~$40M (47%) from China ([Dollar Business](https://in.thedollarbusiness.com/blogs/indias-toy-import-trends-key-countries-to-watch/230403188412)).
- **Imports fell 52% since FY15** due to BIS/QCO certification rules and 70% import duty. **Exports rose 239%.** This is the "Make in India" tailwind — Toyzone (Ahmedabad) and KV Toys India (Thane) are riding it. If you can private-label at Toyzone or a Bhiwandi contract manufacturer, you sidestep import duty entirely + earn the "Made in India" trust badge that Legend of Toys uses in their hero (per PANDAY).
- **Average landed cost per "remote car" via HS-30030 = $2.00 (~₹170)**. Median "stunt car" = $0.90 (~₹75). At your ₹800–₹2,500 retail target, the gross margin band is **₹630–₹2,330 per unit** — even accounting for BIS testing (~₹50K per SKU, one-time), platform commission (20-25% on Amazon, 35% on Meesho), shipping (₹80-150), and returns (8-15% on RC) — there is real room for D2C economics.
