## TL;DR

**There is NO exclusive India distributor for MJX.** Multi-source confirmed.

MJX (Shantou Meijiaxin Toys, China) sells through an "anyone can be a distributor" model on Alibaba/1688 — direct factory-to-buyer with MOQ as low as 1 unit. Every Indian retailer (Daddy Drones, Hobby Central, Bharat Hobby, etc.) is buying independently from China, not through a master importer.

This means **the "India distributor" gap is itself the opportunity.** BCH's three viable paths:

| Path | Speed | MOQ | Setup cost | Per-unit cost | Gross margin |
|------|-------|-----|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **A** Direct China (Alibaba/1688) | 60-90 days | 50-100/SKU | ₹3-5L BIS + shipping | ~₹4,000-5,500 | 60-70% |
| **B** Sub-wholesale from Daddy Drones / Hobby Central | 1 week | 10-20/SKU | ₹0 | ~₹10,000-13,000 | 15-25% |
| **C** MJX HQ direct (preferred-buyer status) | 30-60 days | 100+/SKU | Negotiable | Container pricing | 65-75% |

**Recommended:** Path B for the first 30 days (prove the SKU), then Path A for scale (₹3-5L BIS cert + 100-unit container order).

## SECTION 4 — WHY NO MJX INDIA DISTRIBUTOR EXISTS

This is a deliberate market structure, not an oversight:

| Brand model | Example | Strategy |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Single-arm distribution** (like Kyosho/ARRMA/Futaba) | Hemani Exim = sole India distributor for 13 brands | Premium positioning, controlled pricing, dealer network, slow scale |
| **"Everyone is a distributor"** (like MJX/WLToys/Rlaarlo) | Any Alibaba buyer with $300 can be a "distributor" | Mass-market positioning, race-to-bottom pricing, fast scale, low brand control |

MJX's positioning is **volume + visibility on Instagram** — they don't gate India because every Indian retailer importing $5K of MJX is content marketing for the brand. They benefit from the chaos.

**Implication for BCH:** No relationship-building needed with MJX HQ to get product. Just buy. But also no protection — anyone can undercut you. **The defense is content + retail experience**, not exclusivity.

## SECTION 6 — THE BIS CERTIFICATION REALITY (must read before Path A)

**Mandatory for electric toys imported into India** (since 2020, BIS Notification 21):

| Cost component | Amount |
|----------------|--------|
| BIS application fee per SKU | ₹50,000 - ₹1,00,000 |
| Testing fees (lab, samples) | ₹1,00,000 - ₹2,00,000 |
| Compliance documentation | ₹50,000 - ₹1,00,000 |
| **Total per SKU** | **₹2-4 lakh** |
| Time to certification | 3-4 months |

**Mitigation:** Buy from an importer who ALREADY holds BIS certificates for the MJX SKUs (Daddy Drones, Hobby Central likely do). That's another reason Path B is the right Phase 1 move — you get BIS-compliant product without paying for the cert yourself.

Once volume justifies, get your own BIS cert on 1-2 winning SKUs (₹4-6L total spend for 2 SKUs).
