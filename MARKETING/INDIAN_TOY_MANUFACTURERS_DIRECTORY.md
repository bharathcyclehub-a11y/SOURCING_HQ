# INDIAN TOY MANUFACTURERS — STRATEGIC CALL LIST
**BCH Bangalore | Syed Ibrahim | Compiled: 2026-05-23**
**Goal:** Find Indian toy manufacturers/distributors who either (a) make hobby-grade RC today, or (b) know who does. The supply chain is the moat — once it's clean, marketing scales easily.

---

## THE STRATEGY (why this works)

The Indian toy manufacturing industry is **small and tightly networked**:
- ~6,000 registered manufacturers, but only ~200-300 doing serious volume
- Most cluster physically in 4 zones (Sivakasi, Delhi-NCR, Noida, Channapatna)
- TAITMA (industry body) has < 200 active members
- DPIIT's 7-cluster scheme is mapping the entire ecosystem

**Result:** 5-10 well-targeted calls can map the entire hobby-grade ecosystem. The manufacturer who's NOT doing hobby-grade probably knows the 2-3 people who ARE.

## TIER 1 — INDUSTRY BODIES (call ONE, get 50+ leads)

### A. TAITMA — The All India Toy Manufacturers Association
- **Status:** Primary toy industry body in India
- **Location:** Delhi (head office); regional chapters
- **Website:** taitma.org (intermittent)
- **Search route:** Google "TAITMA Delhi office contact" → IndiaMART listings + LinkedIn often show secretary contact
- **What to ask them:**
  > *"BCH Bangalore — I'm building a hobby-grade RC retail line. Which TAITMA members do brushless RC cars or electronic remote-controlled toys at scale? Can I get the member directory?"*
- **Why this call is gold:** They publish a member directory and host trade meets. One yes from TAITMA = access to all serious Indian toy makers.

### B. DPIIT Toy Promotion Cell (Government of India)
- **Status:** Govt-backed initiative for "Make in India" toys
- **Initiative:** PLI scheme for toys + National Action Plan for Toys (NAPT)
- **7 designated toy clusters under DPIIT:**
  - Channapatna (Karnataka) — traditional wooden toys
  - Varanasi (UP) — wooden + traditional
  - Sivakasi (Tamil Nadu) — plastic + electronic
  - Jaipur (Rajasthan) — wooden + textile
  - Patna (Bihar) — local crafts
  - Goa — emerging
  - Noida/Greater Noida (UP) — electronic + plastic (most relevant for RC)
- **Search route:** Google "DPIIT Toy Promotion Cell contact" + "toy cluster scheme Noida contact"
- **What to ask them:**
  > *"BCH Bangalore — Which manufacturers in the Noida toy cluster are producing brushless electronic toy vehicles? Is there a list of PLI-approved RC toy manufacturers?"*

### C. Toy Association of India (TAI)
- **Status:** Secondary industry body
- **Search route:** "Toy Association of India Mumbai contact"
- **What to ask:** Same as TAITMA — member directory + RC specialty

### D. Indian Toy Federation
- **Status:** Federation of regional toy associations
- **Search route:** "Indian Toy Federation contact secretary"

**Time investment for Tier 1:** ~3-4 phone calls. Output: a member directory + 3-5 referrals to hobby-grade-capable manufacturers.

## TIER 4 — MANUFACTURING CLUSTERS (boots-on-ground research)

When phone calls hit a wall, visiting clusters is the fastest way to find OEM capacity.

### Cluster 1 — Noida / Greater Noida "Toy City"
- **DPIIT designated toy cluster**
- 200+ manufacturers, focus on **electronic + plastic toys**
- This is the cluster MOST LIKELY to have hobby-grade RC capability
- **Visit strategy:** 2-day trip. Phase 5/6 industrial area. Walk shop-to-shop.
- **Anchor contact:** Use Centy Toys (Delhi) or Webby Toys to get introduction to 3-5 Noida manufacturers
- **What to look for:** Brushless motor assembly, 2.4GHz radio component sourcing, LiPo battery integration capability

### Cluster 2 — Sivakasi (Tamil Nadu)
- Massive plastic injection-moulding cluster (also fireworks + matches)
- 1,000+ manufacturers
- Mostly traditional plastic toys
- Less RC capability but huge OEM volume potential for body shells
- **Visit strategy:** 3-day trip via Madurai
- **Use case for BCH:** Plastic body shell + packaging OEM for white-label RC

### Cluster 3 — Bhiwadi (Rajasthan)
- Toyzone factory is here
- Strong plastic moulding + assembly capability
- 50-100 toy manufacturers
- **Visit strategy:** 1-day from Delhi NCR
- **Anchor contact:** Toyzone Impex (+91 88009 49499)

## WHAT MAKES A "STRONG SUPPLY CHAIN" — checklist

Before locking any manufacturer, verify:

| Check | Why |
|-------|-----|
| ✅ GST registered (verify GSTIN online) | Tax compliance, can give input credit |
| ✅ BIS certification for electric toys | Mandatory since 2020 (Notification 21) |
| ✅ ISI mark on products | Safety credential for parents |
| ✅ Minimum 3+ years operating history | Survived COVID, not fly-by-night |
| ✅ Owns the factory (not just trader) | Quality + lead time control |
| ✅ Physical visit done (you've been there) | Catch fakes vs real factories |
| ✅ Sample order delivered + tested | Quality matches the pitch |
| ✅ Reasonable MOQ (≤500 units for hobby SKUs) | Doesn't require ₹50L+ commitment upfront |
| ✅ Clear payment terms (max 50% advance) | Industry standard, anything more = red flag |
| ✅ Written agreement, not just WhatsApp | Legal recourse if dispute |

---

## RED FLAGS (skip these patterns)

| Pattern | Why avoid |
|---------|-----------|
| "Authorized MJX India distributor" claim without proof | Confirmed: no such role exists. Anyone claiming this is a reseller |
| "BIS certified" without cert number | Fake certification — ask for cert number always |
| Cash-only, no invoice | No input credit, no traceability, illegal |
| WhatsApp-only contact, no formal address | Can't visit, can't sue, walk away |
| MOQ <50 units AND no factory tour offered | Likely a trader, not a manufacturer |
| 100% advance demand on first order | Industry standard is 30-50% advance |
