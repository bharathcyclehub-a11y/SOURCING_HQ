## SECTION 8 — The Bigger Pattern (what this dossier reveals)

1. **Three operating eras coexist:**
   - **Old trade (Hemani Exim):** No IG, no LinkedIn, Hotmail addresses, IVR landline. Word-of-mouth only.
   - **Owner-operator retail (MRC, Hobby Central, RC Inc):** WhatsApp + Instagram + one physical shop. Personal relationships dominate.
   - **Modern D2C (Bharat Hobby, Daddy Drones):** Founder-led, content-heavy, social-first. Bharat Hobby on LinkedIn; Daddy Drones on Instagram (981K).

2. **All 6 use WhatsApp except Hemani.** Hemani is the only one with an IVR-only line — they are the gatekeeper *and* the bottleneck.

3. **MJX Hyper Go is the universal bridge SKU.** Every modern operator (Hobby Central, Bharat Hobby, Daddy Drones) leads with it. BCH should make this the hero SKU at launch.

4. **Bharat Hobby has the widest brand catalog (22+ brands) but the youngest legal entity (Feb 2024 incorporation).** They're hungry for retail partners — most receptive to BCH's pitch.

5. **No one runs a public dealer portal.** Every relationship has to be negotiated 1:1. This is BCH's advantage — Bangalore retail with capital + GST + a real shopfront is a rare opener for them.

6. **RC Inc is the only one where the founder is anonymous.** Either they want it that way (enthusiast-collective vibe) or there's a sensitivity around the Traxxas relationship. Approach with respect, not as a sourcing target.
